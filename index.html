How I work
1
Define the decision before choosing the method. I start with what someone needs to do differently — then work backward to the analysis that would actually inform it.
2
Take metrics, assumptions, and uncertainty seriously. The interesting work is usually deciding what to measure and being honest about what the data can't support.
3
Turn messy systems into clear decision paths. I like finding the structure inside complex, ambiguous problems and making it legible.
4
Build analyses stakeholders can actually use. Results land when they're framed in terms of the decision at hand — not just statistical significance.
About

I hold a Master's in Information Management and Systems from UC Berkeley, with a focus on data science. Before Berkeley, I spent three years as an enterprise systems administrator in the Nigerian banking sector — Union Bank, then Heritage Bank — where I learned to ship reliable analytical workflows inside heavily regulated environments.

In summer 2024 I interned at Stripe on a Go-To-Market analytics team, where I refined a lead scoring model and designed experiments to measure conversion-quality trade-offs. Most recently I worked as a Data Governance Analyst at W.I.N Business Solutions, building structured datasets and reporting workflows that reduced manual reconciliation by 25–30%.

I gravitate toward problems where the question itself is ambiguous — figuring out what to measure, what the data can and can't tell you, and how to communicate the answer to people who will make decisions with it.
